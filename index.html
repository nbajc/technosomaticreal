01 / SPATIAL LAYOUT

Resilience
Is A Somatic
Event

This installation functions as a sensory journey within the Hospital of Emotion. Set in a low-height, low-lux chamber, a 40Hz thrum vibrates through the floor, mimicking internal blood flow to silence linguistic "chatter." Bypassing the intellect to hit the nervous system directly (Francis Bacon), the work posits that resilience is a somatic event: the capacity to endure the un-named weight of existence without retreating into fantasy.

Theoretical Framework

Conceptual
Framework

In the psychoanalytic framework of Jacques Lacan and Slavoj Žižek, human experience is mediated by the Symbolic Order—the network of language, laws, and social norms. Before the word exists, there is only the body and the sensation.

I

The Real vs. The Symbolic

When we feel a sensation, society forces us to immediately "name" it. True resilience is not intellectual—it is a biological, proprioceptive reality. The body's ability to maintain integrity against the pressure of the world.

II

Art as Rupture

As Francis Bacon sought to "hit the nervous system directly," this installation bypasses the intellect. Resilience is the capacity to endure the raw, un-named weight of existence without retreating into the safety of fantasy.

III

The Abject Body

Drawing on Kristeva and Cixous, the space critiques the "Phallogocentric" order. Referencing Eva Hesse, the installation validates the Abject—the messy, fluid, heavy reality of the biological body—as the true site of emotional processing.

The Procedure

A Somatic Journey

A linear progression through the materiality of feeling, moving from industrial resistance to organic fragility, and finally to a symbolic "glitch."

01

THE
DIAGNOSIS

Stripping the Symbolic

In a small airlock, a projection commands the abandonment of language: "Your tightness is not 'Anxiety.' Your weight is not 'Grief.' These are names. We are here for the Feeling."

Phase 01 · The Diagnosis · Airlock Projection
02

THE
IMMERSION

The Somatic Gradient
Stage A
INERTIA — THE HEAVY REAL

At a suffocating chokepoint, the visitor forces their mass through black, high-friction boat bumpers on elastic ropes. These blunt "balls of force" read as gravitational forces that refuse to be moved.

High-friction, rubbery, dense. The visitor must physically force their body through the gaps.
Stage B
INFRASTRUCTURE — THE RIGID

Resistance shifts to an entanglement of rigid, ceiling-hung water main pipes that soften into a thicket of garden hoses. Pressure evolves from blunt force into complex entanglement.

Hard, unyielding. Pressure shifts from a "push" to an "entanglement"—rigid societal structures confining fluid emotions.
Stage C · The Hesse Node
THE ABJECT BODY

The path fills with sagging, sand-and-rice-filled lycra forms. This "dead weight" mimics the gravity of skin and fat, forcing a navigation of the body's abject messiness.

Moving through requires embracing the "softness" and "heaviness" of the body—the Abject not as weakness, but as valid material existence.
03

RENCOUNTER
WITH THE REAL

The Exit
Stage D
PNEUMATIC SUSPENSION

The corridor opens into a forest of neutral buoyancy balloons that float perfectly in the zone of human height. Resistance shifts from earth-bound mass to fluid, suspended tension.

Resistance shifts from earth-bound weight to suspended tension. The visitor learns a fluid resilience—pushing through air rather than earth.
Stage E · The REAL
THE EXIT — SENSORY OVERLOAD

The journey terminates in a fractured "glitch" of sensory overload. Glittering Mylar fringes, colorful liquid-filled air hoses, and transparent balloons create an overloaded VOID. This represents the Real (Žižek): a structural glitch in the Symbolic Order that we compulsively attempt to plug with a surplus of fantasies and meanings.

YOUR BODY HAS THE MEMORY.

ENOUGH THINKING.

WELCOME BACK TO THE SIMULATION.

In the Žižekian framework, an orgasm is a structural glitch because it represents a moment where the Symbolic Order—the world of language, social roles, and identity—momentarily collapses into a pure, unmediated somatic intensity. It is the point where "thinking" stops and the body becomes an autonomous site of jouissance (excessive enjoyment).

Primary Examples

The Real
In Five Forms

The "Real" is not reality itself, but the "hard kernel" that resists being captured by words. These examples align with somatic endurance and the abject.

01 Traumatic Event

The Real is that which is "unassimilable." Trauma exists as a hole in the narrative—an interruption that forces reconstruction of one's entire world.

02 The Abject Body

Anything reminding us of our messy biological existence confronts the Real. The Abject reveals we are actually "overstuffed" biological machines.

03 The Gap in the System

A "glitch" isn't just a technical error; it is the Real of the machine breaking through the simulated image. The moment the Symbolic fails to sustain itself.

04 Pain & Physical Labor

Intense physical pain bypasses the intellect entirely. There is no "language" for a muscle screaming under weight; there is only the sensation.

05 The Mystery of the Other — "Che Vuoi?"

The Real manifests as the terrifying uncertainty of what the "Other" wants from us. The transition from the "heavy Real" of physical resistance to the "glimmering Symbolic" of the exit becomes a map of human survival. Our truth is found in the moments when the system fails and the body takes over. ENOUGH THINKING.

The Artist

Natasha
Bajc

Natasha Bajc defines art as a "meeting with the Real as a nervous system bypass"—a visceral provocation that strips away the intellectual safety of language to engage the human body at its most primal level.

As a cancer survivor and expert in resilience, Bajc's work is a profound autopsy of biological endurance. For Bajc, resilience is not a psychological mindset but a somatic event: the capacity to endure the raw, un-named weight of existence without retreating into the safety of fantasy.
